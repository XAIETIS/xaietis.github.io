Motivation

Recent advances in artificial intelligence have led to increasingly complex models that are widely used in decision-making systems across many domains. However, these models often operate as “black boxes,” raising important concerns about transparency, fairness, and accountability. At the same time, modern AI applications rely heavily on large-scale data management systems and frequently involve temporal or continuously evolving data, which introduces additional challenges for reliability and responsible use. This Spring School aims to provide late-stage students and early-career researchers with a comprehensive introduction to the emerging field of explainable and fair AI within modern data systems. By bringing together perspectives from explainable AI, fairness and bias mitigation, and data management, the school will help participants understand how to design, analyze, and deploy AI systems that are both technically robust and socially responsible. The program will combine lectures from leading researchers, hands-on sessions, and opportunities for discussion, allowing participants to deepen their knowledge while building connections with peers and experts in the field. By targeting advanced students and early-career researchers, the Spring School seeks to foster a new generation of scientists equipped to develop trustworthy AI systems grounded in sound data management practices and ethical considerations.
